Vision & Research Positioning

I work at the intersection of optical engineering, remote sensing, and generative AI, with the goal of building AI-native imaging systems that are simultaneously physics-grounded, model-driven, and deployable at scale.

My long-term vision is to design end-to-end pipelines that connect photon capture at the sensor level with perceptual-quality image formation on devices and in the cloud, enabling reliable imaging in challenging real-world conditions.

About & Research Areas

I am a Senior Vision Algorithm Engineer currently working on AI-powered imaging pipelines that integrate optical design, sensor modeling, and modern generative models. I received my Ph.D. in Optical Engineering from the College of Optical Science and Engineering, Zhejiang University, where I was part of the inaugural cohort of the Pujiang National Laboratory Joint Training Ph.D. Program. My work spans both fundamental research and system-level deployment.

Representative topics I continuously work on include:

  • Generative AI for imaging – restoration, enhancement, and synthesis with physically informed models.
  • Remote sensing & push-broom imaging – degradation modeling and jitter-aware restoration for operational satellites.
  • Computational imaging & interferometric systems – deep learning–based image reconstruction for complex optical setups.
  • Low-light & event-based imaging – sim-to-real pipelines and per-scene optimization for video quality.
