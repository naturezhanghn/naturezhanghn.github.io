About Me

I am a Researcher at the Shanghai Scientific Intelligence Research Institute (SAIS), working with Prof. Siyu Zhu on generative model architectures (diffusion transformers, flow matching), model acceleration (quantization, pruning, distillation), and AI for science. I earned my Ph.D. from the College of Optical Science and Engineering, Zhejiang University in June 2025.

Prior to SAIS, I was a Senior Vision Algorithm Engineer at Honor, leading the development of AI-native imaging technologies powered by generative models. I was also a member of the inaugural cohort of the Pujiang National Laboratory Joint Training Ph.D. Program (2022–2025), conducting my doctoral research under the mentorship of Dr. Yueting Chen and co-supervision from Prof. Tianfan Xue. My expertise spans optical engineering and AI-enhanced imaging, with a focus on generative AI and cloud-based computational imaging.

My professional work centers on harnessing generative models to advance imaging algorithms, optimize sensor data interpretation, and design novel computational photography pipelines. I have led and contributed to projects that integrate AI with optics and computer vision, improving image synthesis, enhancement, and real-time processing.

I obtained my B.S. in Optoelectronic Information Science and Engineering from Central South University, where I received the National Scholarship and was recognized as an Outstanding Graduate of Hunan Province. I later pursued an integrated Master’s and Ph.D. program at Zhejiang University; my Master’s research on computational imaging was conducted under the guidance of Prof. Huajun Feng.

During my doctoral studies, I had the privilege to collaborate with Shi Guo, Zhihai Xu, Jinwei Gu, Yihao Liu, Xuelong Li, Qi Li, and Bin Zhao, collectively advancing AI-driven imaging research.

Research Interests

My research focuses on generative AI, model architecture and acceleration, and AI for science. I design algorithms and systems that connect photon capture and sensor readout with learned image formation, enabling high-quality images and videos in challenging conditions such as low light, motion, and complex optics.
