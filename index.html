About Me

I am a Researcher at Shanghai Scientific Intelligence Research Institute (SAIS), advised by Prof. Siyu Zhu. My work focuses on generative model architectures (diffusion transformers, flow matching, and hybrid designs), model acceleration (quantization, pruning, and distillation), and AI for scientific discovery — with applications in computational imaging and inverse problems.

Before joining SAIS, I was a Senior Vision Algorithm Engineer at Honor (until June 2026), leading the development of AI-native imaging pipelines powered by generative models. I received my Ph.D. from Zhejiang University (2025), under the supervision of Dr. Yueting Chen and Prof. Tianfan Xue, as a member of the Pujiang National Laboratory Joint Training Program.

My research spans the full stack: from foundational model design and efficient inference to real-world deployment in camera systems and scientific instruments.

Research Interests

Scalable Generative Models (DiT / Flow) Model Acceleration (Quantization · Pruning · Distillation) AI for Science (Physics-informed ML) Computational Photography & Imaging Efficient Inference & Deployment
